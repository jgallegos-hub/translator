## The positioning (one line)

> *Travel2Chicago is the cinematic cultural-tourism channel that helps real visitors plan meaningful trips to Chicago and Illinois — and feeds into a custom-itinerary service for the people who want a plan built for how they travel.*

The channel is NOT a library channel, NOT an ambient walking channel, NOT a food/bar vlog, NOT a generic tourism listicle. It's cultural discovery (museums, artisan workshops, historic architecture, small-town Illinois, family parks, seasonal concerts, lesser-known cultural spaces) in cinematic format. Libraries are *one* recurring series inside the channel, not the base.

## The sub-niche where we compete

**Cinematic cultural-tourism for planner-intent viewers**, with Chicago as home base and Illinois as the expansion map. The whitespace:

## The 3 most important comment-analysis findings

**1. High-planner-intent channels have 40–60% of their top comments in trip-planning or logistics categories.** Here Be Barr hits 60%. Paolo hits 50%. This is the ratio to aim for. If Travel2Chicago videos get comments like "so beautiful!" but not "is this open on Mondays?" — the content is drifting toward ambient and missing the agency funnel.

**2. Logistics metadata embedded in every video is not optional — it's the conversion infrastructure.** Address + hours + cost + transit + accessibility + best time to visit. Pinned first comment of every video. Comments show 20–28% of viewers ask these questions; giving the answers proactively is trust-building.

**3. The CTA for the travel agency has to be soft, helper-positioned, and never interrupt content.** Paolo's "Want a guide? We work with Nori. Contact us." is the template. Hard-sell kills the channel's vibe. The funnel works via aggregate trust across 40+ videos, not via one pitch.

## 3 decisions for Abraham this week (updated)

### Decision 1 — Travel-agency service readiness
**Why it matters:** The channel's differentiator is the travel agency behind it. If the agency isn't ready to receive inquiries by video 3, the funnel leaks. Need: a simple landing page (travel2chicago.com or equivalent) with "Request a custom itinerary" form, a pricing concept (tiered by trip length), and a response workflow (who answers, in how long).

**Recommendation:** Landing page + form live BEFORE video 1 ships. Even if the "agency" is just Abraham answering emails for the first 20 clients, that's fine. But the *infrastructure* must be ready.

### Decision 2 — Content modes to lock in first
**Why it matters:** The agency's custom-itinerary service has multiple modes (cultural, museum, nature, nocturno, scientific, family). The content calendar should reflect each mode over time. But launching with all 6 dilutes the message.

**Recommendation:** Launch with **cultural + architecture + hidden gems** as the defining voice. Museum and family can come in month 3. Nocturno in month 6 (by Q4, high CPM). Nature in year 2.

### Decision 3 — Script writing workflow for ElevenLabs narration
**Why it matters:** Weekly cadence + cinematic cultural content + cloned-voice narration = writing discipline is now the main production bottleneck. A 14-minute video needs ~1,800–2,200 words of script, written for spoken delivery, not reading.

**Recommendation:** Lock a script template — hook (20 sec) → setup (90 sec) → 3 development blocks (3 min each) → climax (2 min) → CTA (30 sec). Same skeleton for every essay video. Saves hours of re-writing.
