# 01 — Niche Map: Where Travel2Chicago Should Compete

**Channel:** Travel2Chicago — faceless cinematic, Chicago + Illinois libraries + architecture
**Audience target:** US-based, English-speaking, travel/architecture/library enthusiasts
**Date:** 2026-04-16

---

## Executive view

Travel2Chicago sits at the intersection of 6 overlapping sub-niches. Not all are equally attractive. The right play is to **lead with two high-affinity, under-contested sub-niches** (cinematic urban ambience + libraries/quiet-places) and use Chicago as the geographic "home base" that grounds the brand before expanding to other US cities. Fighting head-on in the saturated generic "things to do in Chicago" space is a losing game — the channel's edge is cinematic atmosphere + expertise in an under-covered aesthetic (libraries, architecture, quiet urban beauty).

---

## Sub-niche analysis

### 1. Cinematic faceless travel / walking tours (Nomadic Ambience, Rambalac, Watched Walker, ProWalk Tours style)

- **Estimated demand:** High. Top channels in this space reach 500K–1.5M subscribers, with per-video views in the 1M–5M range for outliers. Long-form (45–90 min) immersion videos have strong evergreen watch-time.
- **Competition:** Medium–high globally, but **low for Chicago/Midwest specifically**. Almost every heavy hitter is Japan, Korea, London, Paris, or Europe focused. Chicago is effectively open territory.
- **Saturation:** Low for Midwest US; high for Tokyo/London/Paris.
- **CPM (estimated, US audience):** $3–$8, leaning $5–$7 because of high commercial-intent audiences (people planning trips).
- **Affinity with our angle:** Very high. Faceless cinematic, long-form, ambient-first — this is the DNA we're copying.
- **Verdict:** ✅ **Core niche.** Compete here but with a geographic moat (Chicago + Midwest).

### 2. Hidden gems / "secret places" travel

- **Estimated demand:** Medium–high. Strong search volume ("hidden gems Chicago", "secret places Chicago") and YouTube ecosystem. Driven by discovery-minded travelers and locals.
- **Competition:** Medium. Many "5 hidden gems" listicle creators exist but few with cinematic, single-video-per-place treatment.
- **Saturation:** Medium for Chicago. Lots of short vlog-style listicles, few cinematic 10–15 min dives on one specific place.
- **CPM:** Same range as travel ($3–$8, leaning mid).
- **Affinity:** High. Fits a library-focused channel naturally — libraries ARE hidden gems for most travelers.
- **Verdict:** ✅ **Secondary attack vector.** Use as a title/framing device for library and architecture content ("The library Chicago doesn't want you to find" type framing — carefully, avoid clickbait).

### 3. Architecture / building essays (Stewart Hicks, DamiLee, The B1M)

- **Estimated demand:** High and growing. DamiLee hit 2M subscribers in ~5 years; The B1M is at 3.9M; Stewart Hicks at 670K. Audience is educated, high-income — excellent for premium CPM.
- **Competition:** High at the top. Saturated with general architecture essays. BUT — most architecture channels are global; very few own "Chicago architecture" as a signature, and Stewart Hicks (the main Chicago architecture creator) is on-camera and academic in format.
- **Saturation:** Low for cinematic-faceless architecture. High for on-camera educational architecture.
- **CPM (estimated):** $4–$10. Architecture viewers skew older, higher income, more commercial intent (home design, luxury travel, credit cards, hotels).
- **Affinity:** Very high. Chicago IS the architecture city (birthplace of the skyscraper, Mies van der Rohe, Sullivan, Wright in Oak Park, Marina City, the Loop).
- **Verdict:** ✅ **Core niche.** Claim "cinematic faceless Chicago architecture" — no one owns it.

### 4. Libraries / quiet places / study spots / dark academia aesthetic

- **Estimated demand:** Low–medium volume in raw search, but a **hyper-loyal, under-served** audience. Library ambience videos pull 100K–500K views on small channels. "Dark academia" and "aesthetic study spots" are trending categories on TikTok/Instagram that haven't fully crossed to YouTube long-form.
- **Competition:** Very low. Timothy Kenny does library deep-dives with no serious competition. There's no signature faceless-cinematic library channel.
- **Saturation:** Very low. This is the biggest gap in the whole map.
- **CPM:** Lower than travel ($2–$5 estimated), but monetization upside from niche sponsorships (Audible, Blinkist, Masterclass, productivity apps, reading/journaling brands).
- **Affinity:** Perfect. This is the channel's defensible moat. No one else is doing "cinematic library cinema" at scale.
- **Verdict:** ✅ **Signature niche / moat.** This is what makes Travel2Chicago different from 500 other travel channels.

### 5. Quiet / aesthetic / ambient travel (overlapping w/ faceless cinematic but slower, mood-first)

- **Estimated demand:** Medium. Overlaps heavily with #1 and #4. ASMR rain walks, snow walks, foggy streets.
- **Competition:** Medium globally. Low for Chicago/Midwest.
- **Saturation:** Low for Chicago.
- **CPM:** $2–$5 (lower because some ASMR viewers have ad-blockers, and ambience videos are often left playing in background — YouTube serves fewer ads).
- **Affinity:** High. Natural extension of cinematic content.
- **Verdict:** ✅ **Tertiary mode.** Use occasionally for retention-heavy long-form (a 60-min "Chicago rainy night walk, 4K ambience" drops every quarter).

### 6. Generic "things to do in Chicago" travel

- **Estimated demand:** Very high (raw search volume).
- **Competition:** Very high. Every travel creator who has spent 48 hours in Chicago has posted "10 things to do in Chicago."
- **Saturation:** Extreme.
- **CPM:** Mid-range ($3–$6).
- **Affinity:** Low for our angle. This is where we do NOT want to compete directly with Mark Wiens-style vlog creators.
- **Verdict:** ❌ **Avoid as primary focus.** Only attack via long-tail side doors: "best Chicago things to do for architecture lovers", "Chicago for readers", "quiet Chicago", etc. Do not make generic "top 10 Chicago" videos.

## Where to compete first (90-day focus)

Three sub-niches, in priority order:

### Priority 1 — Cinematic faceless Chicago architecture (Sub-niches 1 + 3)

Claim the "Chicago architecture in cinematic, faceless, long-form format" territory. No serious competitor. Stewart Hicks is on-camera essay; walking-tour creators don't have architectural depth. We fuse both: cinematic walk + just enough narrated architectural context (via subtitles or sparing voiceover).

**Why this wins first:** Chicago IS known for architecture. Searchability is built in. The content stands out aesthetically vs. all other "Chicago travel" videos.

### Priority 2 — Libraries and quiet places as signature moat (Sub-niche 4)

Establish "the channel that treats libraries like cathedrals." Start in Chicago (Harold Washington Library, Chicago Cultural Center ex-library, Newberry Library, UChicago Harper/Regenstein), then expand across Illinois (the Oak Park Library, small-town Carnegie libraries), then US (Boston Public Library, LA Central, Library of Congress).

**Why this wins medium-term:** No competition. Hyper-loyal niche audience (readers, students, introverts, academics). Strong repeat-viewing. Monetization from intellectual-audience sponsors.

### Priority 3 — Hidden gems framing (Sub-niche 2)

Not a niche to build the brand on, but a powerful **title/packaging device** to surface libraries, architecture, and quiet places to broader audiences. "The Chicago library most tourists never see" is more clickable than "Chicago Cultural Center tour".

**Why this wins for reach:** Hidden gems framing drives impressions; libraries/architecture delivers retention.

---

## Sub-niches to deprioritize

- **Generic Chicago tourism** (vlog-style) — oversaturated, misaligned with our aesthetic.
- **Food tourism** — someone else's moat (Chowcago, Kimchi & Beans own it locally).
- **Sports / Chicago culture vlogging** — not our strength.

These don't disappear forever; they could become B-roll or future spin-off series once the main channel is established.

---

## Summary of positioning

> **Travel2Chicago = the cinematic faceless channel for people who find beauty in quiet places — libraries, historic architecture, and the spaces most tourists walk past.**

This positioning gives us three things:
1. A defensible niche (libraries + Chicago, no competitor owns this).
2. An aesthetic moat (cinematic faceless, ambient-first, long-form).
3. A pathway to scale (expand from Chicago → Illinois → Midwest → US cities for architecture and libraries, keeping the format and brand coherent).

---

## CPM reality check

Blended CPM expectation for Travel2Chicago, US-audience-focused, year 1: **$4–$7.** RPM after YouTube's 45% cut: **$2.20–$3.85 per 1,000 views.** Seasonal variance: Q4 spikes +40–75% vs. Q1 due to holiday travel advertising. Actionable implication: front-load architecture + library content in Q1 (lower CPM, but builds audience without wasting peak ad-revenue windows); save headline city tours and "bucket list Chicago" style content for Q3–Q4.
