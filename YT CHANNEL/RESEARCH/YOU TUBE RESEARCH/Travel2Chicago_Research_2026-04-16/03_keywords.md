# 03 — Keyword Research

**Purpose:** 45 keywords mapped across 6 categories, with the top 10 for the launch video marked. Volume/competition ratings are based on YouTube autocomplete patterns, Google Trends signals, article coverage, and relative SERP saturation. Treat them as directional, not precise search volumes.

---

## How to read this

- **Volume:** High / Medium / Low — relative to the Chicago-travel ecosystem.
- **Competition:** High / Medium / Low — how saturated YouTube SERP is.
- **Intent:** Informational (learn), Inspirational (dream), Planning (decide).
- **Evergreen / Seasonal:** year-round vs. peaks (summer = Q2–Q3, holidays = Q4).
- **Long-tail:** can spawn multiple child videos.

## Title construction cheat sheet (from keyword research)

Build titles around three slots — (A) **what**, (B) **where/when**, (C) **why it matters/framing**:

- `[Cinematic action] [specific neighborhood] [specific time/weather]` → walking-tour mode ("Walking Chicago's Loop at sunrise — 4K, no narration")
- `Why [specific Chicago landmark] is [superlative]` → essay mode ("Why Chicago's oldest library is still its most beautiful")
- `The [hidden/quiet/forgotten] [Chicago thing] most tourists never [verb]` → hidden-gems packaging ("The Chicago library most tourists never enter")
- `[First-person experience] + [unique Chicago detail]` → narrative mode ("I spent a day in Chicago's most beautiful room")

Mix and match. The title patterns from Solo Solo Travel and Stewart Hicks (narrative hook + specific subject) have the highest outlier rate in our benchmark set.

## Seasonal planning

| Quarter | Keyword focus | Video angle |
|---------|---------------|-------------|
| Q1 (Jan–Mar) | Libraries, architecture interiors, study spots, Chicago in winter | Indoor + cozy + ambient. Low CPM but builds audience. |
| Q2 (Apr–Jun) | Riverwalk, street art, 606 Trail, Oak Park (Frank Lloyd Wright) | Shoulder season. Outdoor content starts. |
| Q3 (Jul–Sep) | Lake Michigan, outdoor walks, Illinois day trips, sunset/night walks | Peak travel searches + high CPM. Launch biggest outlier attempts here. |
| Q4 (Oct–Dec) | Christmas / holiday lights, Chicago at night, "best of" comparatives | Highest CPM. Holiday travel advertiser spend peaks. |

## Things we are NOT chasing (on purpose)

- "Chicago food" / "best deep dish" — someone else's moat.
- "Chicago sports" — not our brand.
- "Chicago crime / safety" — wrong audience signal.
- "Cheapest things to do Chicago" — low CPM audience.
