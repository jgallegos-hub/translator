# 05 — First Video Recommendation

**Goal:** Launch Travel2Chicago with a video that has legitimate outlier potential (target: 100K+ views in first 90 days), establishes the channel's signature aesthetic, and can be produced realistically by a solo creator based in Glen Ellyn.

Three proposals follow. Each is designed to be produceable in **2–3 shooting days + 5–7 edit days** and to give YouTube's algorithm the clearest possible signal of what this channel is.

At the end, one is recommended.

---

## Proposal A — "The Most Beautiful Room in Chicago (That Almost Nobody Visits)"

**One-line premise:** A cinematic walk through Preston Bradley Hall at the Chicago Cultural Center — the room housing the world's largest Tiffany dome — framed as a hidden gem.

### Title variants (A/B testable)
- **Variant A (recommended):** "The Most Beautiful Room in Chicago (That Almost Nobody Visits)"
- **Variant B:** "Chicago's Secret Tiffany Dome — The Most Beautiful Room in America?"
- **Variant C:** "I Spent a Day Inside Chicago's Most Overlooked Masterpiece"

### Thumbnail concept
- **Composition:** Low angle, looking up at the stained-glass Tiffany dome filling 70% of the frame. The blue and amber stained glass dominates.
- **Palette:** Deep blue + warm amber + bright white (the dome's natural colors).
- **Text overlay (minimal):** Small, bottom-left: "CHICAGO • 1897" in thin white serif. No other text.
- **Secondary option:** Split thumbnail — half empty hall (wide), half close-up of the dome — with a vertical dividing line. More dramatic if we're A/B testing.

### Duration
**12–15 min.** Essay-hybrid mode (cinematic b-roll + minimal captions, optional light voiceover).

### Structure by block

| Block | Time | Content |
|-------|------|---------|
| Hook | 0:00–0:15 | Cold open. Three fast cuts: exterior of the Cultural Center, stepping into the marble stairwell, first glimpse of the dome. No title card. On-screen caption: "The most beautiful room in Chicago is free. Almost no one visits it." |
| Setup | 0:15–1:30 | Slow reveal of Preston Bradley Hall. The walk up the grand staircase. First full-frame shot of the dome. Short captioned context: "Built 1897. Original central library of Chicago." |
| Development 1 | 1:30–5:00 | The dome itself. Multiple angles, slow pans. Context captions: scale (38 feet across), 30,000 pieces of glass, Louis Comfort Tiffany + Healy & Millet. |
| Development 2 | 5:00–9:00 | The wider building — the other dome (G.A.R. Memorial Hall), the grand staircases, mosaic inlays, reading rooms. Weave in the library-to-cultural-center history. |
| Climax | 9:00–11:30 | Return to Preston Bradley. Time-lapse of sunlight moving across the dome as afternoon falls. Silence. Let the room be the closer. |
| Cierre + CTA | 11:30–12:30 | One caption: "Chicago Cultural Center. 78 E. Washington St. Free to enter." Channel handle and subscribe prompt. No hype, no "smash the like button." |

### Locations to film (with addresses)
- **Chicago Cultural Center** — 78 E. Washington St., Chicago, IL 60602
  - Preston Bradley Hall (3rd floor, south side)
  - G.A.R. Rotunda (2nd floor, north side)
  - Grand staircases (both sides)
  - Yates Gallery (2nd floor)
- **Exterior establishing shots** — corner of Michigan + Washington, morning light.

### Keywords targeted
- **Primary:** "Chicago Cultural Center", "Tiffany dome Chicago", "Preston Bradley Hall"
- **Secondary:** "hidden gems Chicago", "most beautiful building Chicago", "Chicago architecture", "Chicago libraries"
- **Long-tail:** "things to do in Chicago free", "Chicago architecture hidden"

### Music mood suggested
Cinematic ambient-classical. References: Max Richter's "On the Nature of Daylight" mood (not the actual track — use royalty-free alternatives in Artlist or Epidemic Sound under "reflective," "cinematic minimalism," or "neo-classical"). Single piece across the whole video, building slightly toward the 9-minute mark.

### Why this has outlier potential
1. **Topic is almost zero-competition on YouTube.** Most Chicago videos skip the Cultural Center for the Bean and Navy Pier.
2. **Built-in "didn't know that" hook** — world's largest Tiffany dome is a genuinely surprising fact. This is the Matt Par style knowledge gap.
3. **Free-to-film, accessible all year** (indoor — Q1-safe).
4. **Clean testable framing** — "most beautiful room that nobody visits" is pure hidden-gem packaging.
5. **Visually stunning** — the dome alone can carry 3 minutes of retention.

### Risk / caveats
- Cultural Center rules on tripod / filming — need to verify policy (usually fine for handheld + stabilizer, no drones indoors).
- Sunlight through the dome is unpredictable; requires 2–3 visits to get good light.

## Proposal B — "Walking Chicago's Loop at Night in the Rain (4K, No Narration)"

**One-line premise:** A pure ambient cinematic walk from the Chicago River up through the Loop, then down Michigan Avenue to Millennium Park, on a rainy October or November evening. Faceless, voiceless, 4K, captions only for location markers.

### Title variants
- **Variant A (recommended):** "Walking Chicago's Loop at Night in the Rain (4K, No Narration)"
- **Variant B:** "Rainy Chicago Night Walk — The Loop to Millennium Park (4K Ambience)"
- **Variant C:** "Chicago in the Rain — A 45-Minute Walk Through the Loop (4K, Silent)"

### Thumbnail concept
- **Composition:** Street-level view down a wet Chicago street (State Street or LaSalle). L-train tracks above, neon reflections on the pavement below, a single yellow cab or red traffic light as color pop.
- **Palette:** Deep blue-black wet pavement, warm yellow streetlights, red-amber from signage.
- **Text:** None. Or a small "CHICAGO | RAIN | 4K" in bottom-right, thin white, 12pt equivalent.

### Duration
**45–60 min.** Ambient mode — long-form for watch-time, zero narration, ambient sound only.

### Structure by block

| Block | Time | Content |
|-------|------|---------|
| Hook | 0:00–0:15 | Camera already walking. First shot: stepping off a bridge over the Chicago River. Wet pavement, L train passing overhead. Ambient sound + rain. No title. |
| Walk — River to Loop | 0:15–15:00 | From State Street Bridge, up State through the Loop. The Theater District lights. Marshall Field's / Macy's building. L tracks. Crowds with umbrellas. |
| Walk — Loop interior | 15:00–30:00 | Off the main drag. Monadnock Building, Rookery Building, LaSalle canyon. Quieter streets. More architecture visible. |
| Walk — toward the park | 30:00–45:00 | Michigan Avenue. Art Institute lions. Cloud Gate in Millennium Park (from a distance, in rain and night). |
| Cierre | 45:00–46:00 | Stop walking. Static shot of the city from the Lurie Garden or the Pritzker Pavilion. Rain continues. Fade. |

### Locations to film
- **State Street Bridge** (over the Chicago River) — starting point.
- **State Street** through the Loop (Madison, Monroe, Adams).
- **LaSalle Street** (for the financial district's "canyon" shots).
- **Monadnock Building** (53 W. Jackson) and the **Rookery** (209 S. LaSalle) — architectural legends.
- **Art Institute of Chicago** (111 S. Michigan) — the lions + front steps.
- **Millennium Park** — Cloud Gate, Pritzker Pavilion (end point).

### Keywords targeted
- **Primary:** "Chicago walking tour", "Chicago at night", "Chicago rain walk"
- **Secondary:** "Chicago 4K walk", "Chicago downtown walk", "Chicago Loop"
- **Long-tail:** "Chicago walking tour no narration", "Chicago ambient walk"

### Music mood
**No music.** Pure ambient rain + city. Field recording is the soundtrack. This is the format that made Watched Walker and Rambalac.

### Why this has outlier potential
1. **Fits the proven "walking + city + night + rain + 4K" formula** (Rambalac, Watched Walker, Seoul Walker) which consistently delivers 2–5x outliers.
2. **Chicago in rain is under-documented on YouTube.** Search "Chicago rain walk" — sparse serious long-form.
3. **Evergreen** — viewers watch this for sleep, study, work background. Long-tail watch hours are massive.
4. **Algorithm-friendly** — 45-minute average watch time is gold for the algorithm.

### Risk / caveats
- Weather dependency — need to chase a specific rainy evening, which may take 2–6 weeks of waiting.
- Solo walking with a gimbal for 45 min requires stamina + battery management.
- Equipment (good gimbal, low-light 4K camera, dead cat windscreen for mic) is non-trivial.

## Proposal C — "Why Chicago Has the Most Beautiful Library in America"

**One-line premise:** An essay-format cinematic deep-dive on Harold Washington Library (or, alternative: the Newberry Library), framed around the claim that it's the most beautiful library in the US and why nobody talks about it.

### Title variants
- **Variant A (recommended):** "Why Chicago Has the Most Beautiful Library in America"
- **Variant B:** "Chicago Built the Largest Public Library in the World — And Then Forgot It Existed"
- **Variant C:** "The 10-Story Library in Downtown Chicago That Most Tourists Miss"

### Thumbnail concept
- **Composition:** The Harold Washington Library exterior at dusk, the five giant bronze owls (Raymond Kaskey sculptures) on the parapet dramatically lit, with a blue-hour sky behind.
- **Palette:** Deep blue sky + warm bronze + red brick facade.
- **Text overlay:** "CHICAGO'S BIGGEST SECRET" in bold white, upper-right. Or split thumbnail with "Before / After" — exterior vs. the Winter Garden interior.

### Duration
**13–16 min.** Essay mode with cinematic b-roll, optional voiceover (if Abraham prefers voiceless, use captions throughout — the Solo Solo Travel model).

### Structure by block

| Block | Time | Content |
|-------|------|---------|
| Hook | 0:00–0:30 | "In 1991, Chicago opened the largest public library in the world. Today, almost no tourist has heard of it." Fast cuts: exterior, interior, the Winter Garden, the owls. |
| Setup | 0:30–2:00 | Context: why Chicago needed a new library in the 80s, the architectural competition, why Thomas Beeby won. Archival images. |
| Development 1 | 2:00–6:00 | Walking in. The scale. Floor by floor tour. Stop on the 9th floor — the Winter Garden. |
| Development 2 | 6:00–10:00 | The owls. The mosaics. The special collections. The rare book room. Each with cinematic b-roll + captions. |
| Climax | 10:00–12:30 | Return to Winter Garden at sunset. Slow tilt-up to the glass ceiling. City skyline visible. Final reveal of what the space feels like at golden hour. |
| Cierre + CTA | 12:30–14:00 | "400 S. State Street. Free. Open to everyone." Invite to subscribe for more overlooked Chicago architecture. |

### Locations
- **Harold Washington Library** — 400 S. State St., Chicago, IL 60605
  - Exterior (all 4 sides + owl detail)
  - Winter Garden (9th floor)
  - Grand staircase
  - Special collections / rare book room (requires staff request but usually allowed)
- **Alternative pivot if Harold Washington filming is restricted:** The **Newberry Library** (60 W. Walton) — privately funded, stunning 1893 Henry Ives Cobb Romanesque building, easier permission.

### Keywords targeted
- **Primary:** "Harold Washington Library", "Chicago libraries", "most beautiful library America"
- **Secondary:** "Chicago hidden gems", "Chicago architecture", "Chicago Cultural Center"
- **Long-tail:** "Chicago library tour", "largest library in America"

### Music mood
Cinematic + reflective. Slightly more uplifting than Proposal A, building to a warm resolve in the final block. Alternatively: no music, pure ambient Harold Washington sound (the soft murmur of a working library has its own texture).

### Why this has outlier potential
1. **Zero direct competition.** No cinematic long-form video of Harold Washington exists at scale.
2. **The "most beautiful X in America" framing + "nobody talks about it"** is the Stewart Hicks + Matt Par formula combined.
3. **Library niche sets the channel up for sequels** — Newberry, UChicago Regenstein, Oak Park, Ravenswood, Pullman. Every video funnels viewers to the next.
4. **Strong shareability** — readers, academics, architects, Chicagoans who've been there but never realized how special it is.

### Risk / caveats
- Library filming permissions — public library may require a notice; some areas restrict tripods. Verify before scheduling.
- The exterior is architecturally divisive (post-modern). Need excellent shot composition to make it look as beautiful as the claim.

## Recommended launch: **Proposal A — The Cultural Center / Tiffany Dome video**

### Why A over B and C

1. **Lowest risk, highest signal clarity.** The Cultural Center is accessible, indoor, year-round. Abraham can produce this before summer without waiting for weather.

2. **Perfect brand-defining first video.** It hits all three pillars of Travel2Chicago simultaneously: architecture, quiet places (libraries/cultural spaces), cinematic hidden-gem packaging. Anyone who watches 12 minutes of this understands what the channel is and wants more.

3. **Outlier mechanics in place:**
   - Extreme specificity ("world's largest Tiffany dome").
   - Visual reveal paced (dome is held for 3+ min in the climax).
   - "Most beautiful room that nobody visits" — a packaging line that travels well (clippable, shareable).
   - Strong thumbnail potential (the dome is already a thumbnail by itself).

4. **Lowest production barrier.** One location. One shooting day (with a reshoot for better light as a bonus). Accessible. No drones needed, no tripod restrictions that are unusual.

5. **Best sequel ladder.** A strong opener on the Cultural Center establishes the channel's voice. Video 2 can then be Harold Washington (Proposal C), Video 3 can be the Newberry, Video 4 can be the Rainy Loop walk (Proposal B), etc. The Cultural Center is the most efficient proof-of-concept, and everything else compounds from it.

### Proposal B is the best Q4 play
Save Proposal B for October/November when Chicago gets reliably rainy evenings and the weather can be chased deliberately. The "Rainy Loop" video should be videos 4–6 on the channel, not video 1.

### Proposal C is the best Q2 play
Harold Washington in spring/summer with golden Winter Garden light = ideal. Ideally video 2 or video 3.

## Production notes (for whichever launches first)

- **Shoot in 4K, deliver 4K, even if uploading 1080p initially.** Future-proof the footage.
- **Gimbal is mandatory.** Ronin / Crane Plus or equivalent. Shaky handheld breaks the cinematic promise.
- **Record native sound** even if you don't use it — you'll thank yourself in edit.
- **B-roll to A-roll ratio: 5:1.** Over-shoot. 30 minutes of footage for every 5 minutes of final video.
- **Caption plan locked before editing.** Decide voiceover vs. silent-with-captions before you shoot — it changes the pacing.
- **Thumbnail: 3 options, A/B test.** YouTube's native thumbnail test tool is available inside Studio.
- **Upload day: Thursday 4–6 PM CT** for a US audience (per Watched Walker's published schedule, this is a high-engagement window).
